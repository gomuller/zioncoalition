POSSESSION / REST CONNECTION

As Jesus builds My Jesus-Greg WORLD in the JesusVerse (and I, often possessed, do help Him) I notice Jesus is creating a Thesaurus in my mind --- so that all of these phrases point (me) toward the same mysterious human experience:

"Lose yourself in the music."

"Lose track of time."

"Get caught up in the work."

"Be absorbed."

"Be carried away."

"Be beside yourself with joy."

"Be filled with the Spirit."

"Rest in Christ."

"Pray without ceasing."

"Nephi did praise the Lord all the day long."

From one angle, "losing yourself" sounds dangerous.

My Jesus has me run with sizzors. We worry when someone says, "Greg has really lost it."

But from another angle, some of the most meaningful experiences in life involve a kind of holy self-forgetfulness.

When a musician becomes one with the song.

When a music listener does the same.

When an athlete enters "the zone."

When the fans see “nothing but net”

‘When the moon hits your eye like a big pizza pie’

When a scientist loses track of time pursuing discovery.

When a layman loses track of time learning about discovery.

When a mother forgets herself caring for a child.

When an infant latches on to breast-filled bliss.

When enslaved people sang spirituals that carried them through unbearable suffering.

When a disciple becomes so focused on Christ that self-consciousness fades into the background.

Jesus seems to point (me) toward (possesses me with) a paradox:

"For whosoever will save his life shall lose it: and whosoever will lose his life (even for A moment) for my sake shall find it (even for THAT moment)."

Not annihilation of the self.

Discovery of the deeper self.

And RE-discovery, and again, RE-discovery, and again.

The smaller self—the anxious self, the image-managing self, the constantly self-referencing self—begins to loosen its grip.

Nipple slip on Babylon’s Breast.

Something larger takes hold.

This may be why Jesus also says:

"Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."

The rest may not simply be physical rest. It may be relief from the exhausting burden of carrying ourselves by ourselves.

In that state, a person can sing while picking cotton.

In that state, the ‘Chariot of God can swing low, and carry you’.

Paul and Silas can sing in prison.

Nephi can praise God "all the day long."

A worshipper can kneel at the sacrament table.

A believer can become so focused on Christ that hours pass like minutes.

The world often describes these moments as flow, absorption, immersion, or being in the zone.

Scripture often describes them as being filled with the Spirit, abiding in Christ, receiving rest, rejoicing, praising, or having the love of God shed abroad in the heart.

Different vocabulary.

Perhaps a similar underlying phenomenon.

The irony is that we spend much of life trying to find ourselves by thinking about ourselves.

Jesus proposes something almost opposite:

Lose yourself in something higher.

Lose yourself in love.

Lose yourself in service.

Lose yourself in worship.

Lose yourself in Christ.

And there, paradoxically, you may finally find yourself.

“Greg”, Jesus says, “You and I are building a whole new world where you can m.o.r.e. e.a.s.i.l.y. lose yourself. We will call it our Jesus-Greg WORLD in the JesusVerse.”
