The Hidden World That Holds Me Up

Zion Coalition is part of an elaborate and complex system that is necessary for me. And probably only me. Most great systems are vast, intricate, and largely invisible to the people they serve.

Examples of Hidden Systems

Universities: most students never need to understand the whole machinery. They simply show up, learn, and move forward.

Hospitals: trauma patients need healing, not the whole supply chain and credentialing structure.

Aviation: passengers need only trust the system carrying them safely.

Movies — especially The Chosen: the crew can do their part, while the director carries the whole invisible world.

First Drafts, Hidden Inheritances, and the Mechanism of Zion-Building

One of the great misunderstandings of our age is the belief that our best work is already behind us. But Jesus sees differently. Zion Coalition exists to reveal this difference.

Consider Axl Rose. November Rain still gets airplay, still moves people, still echoes with that unmistakable ache. But Heaven sees more: November Rain wasn’t a peak — it was a foundation.

Believe that the first version was only the first version.
Believe that God intends to build on it.
Believe that what came before was a beginning, not an ending.

Why We Struggle to Believe Our First Draft Isn’t Our Last

1. The world glorifies finished products, not living processes.

Culture worships polish, hiding drafts, rehearsals, awkward beginnings.

2. Most people misinterpret their hidden worlds.

The pressure they feel isn’t failure — it’s unfinished inheritance.

3. Early Zion feels too humble to be holy.

The beginnings feel small and unimpressive. But Jesus always starts in the low places.

4. No one ever told them the truth: the draft is the evidence.

Your early version was proof you were chosen — not proof that you peaked.

5. People fear hope more than failure.

Expectation hurts. But belief opens the door for Jesus to entrust the next draft.

Gift Of Hunger For Gifts

Most people today don’t know how receiving actually works. Because receiving anything real requires two gifts, not one.

Most people think a gift is just the thing itself — like the Book of Mormon, or a redeemed classic rock song, or a vision, or an insight whispered by the Spirit.

The second part of the gift is the hunger for the gift.

The readiness. The inner permission. The ache. The desire. The spiritual oxygen that allows the gift to ignite.

A Gift Without Hunger Is Like...

  • a toy without batteries
  • a car without wheels
  • a seed given to someone who has no soil
  • a treasure map handed to someone too exhausted to care
  • a violin with no strings
  • a candle with no flame
  • a letter with no address
  • a key with no door
  • a kite with no wind
  • bread with no hunger to receive it
  • a well with no bucket
  • rain falling on stone instead of soil
  • a compass with no north
  • a lamp with no oil
  • a fire with no warmth
  • a bridge that leads nowhere
  • a song with no listener
  • a river with no outlet
  • a feast with no guests
  • a gift left wrapped forever

The Four-Gift Matrix

#4 — The hidden structure that prepares a person

The orchestrations, the experiences, the shaping of the heart. The soil.

#3 — The gift of hunger

The inner longing that makes a person ready. The oxygen.

#2 — The actual gift

The thing itself — a scripture, a song reborn, a vision, an insight. The spark.

#1 — The feeling the gift produces in full measure

The fire. The transformation. The joy. The flame.

People think the magic is in the spark.
But the magic is actually in the oxygen.

Jesus Orchestrates Both Sides

Jesus must orchestrate both the gift and the hunger. If He gives the gift but not the hunger, the person cannot receive it. If He gives hunger but not the gift, the person aches without relief.

Jesus is not only the Giver —
He is the One who prepares you to receive.
