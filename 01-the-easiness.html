The Easiness

Somewhere late in the JesusVerse — so late it loops back to the beginning — the exhausted disciple finally asks: “Jesus… how is Your yoke easy?”

The River

Jesus walks us down to a quiet river — not the Jordan, not the Mississippi, something older. Something that feels like it was here before Eden and will flow long after Zion rises in full.

He hands us a little raft. Not a motorboat. Not a canoe. A Tom-Sawyer-grade, Huck-Finn-primitive raft — just enough to hold a person and a calling.

“Push off.”

We step on. We wobble — then settle. The river moves all by itself.

1. The River Has Its Own Power

You don’t propel it. You don’t push it. You don’t drag it. You align with it.

Swartz & Loehr would call it “full engagement.” Rush’s Limelight would call it “getting on with the fascination.” Physics would call it “path of least resistance.” Jesus calls it:

“My Spirit.”

2. The Burden Is Light Because It’s Not Really Yours

“A burden is only heavy when you pick it up alone. A yoke is only painful when you fight the direction.”

When you try to row your life, you exhaust yourself. When you stand still and let the river carry you, you rest while you move.

Stillness that travels. Rest that accelerates. Surrender that becomes power.

3. The Real Relation

The real fascination is not the raft. Not the riverbanks. Not even the rapids.

The real relation. The underlying theme: relating to Him while the river does the heavy lifting.

It was never “work for Jesus.” It was always “work with Jesus.” And eventually: “Jesus works through you.”

4. The How of Easy

  • Stop rowing.
  • Stop forcing outcomes He never asked you to control.
  • Tune to His energy, not your anxiety.
  • Follow the path of least divine resistance.
  • Let fascination replace willpower.

The Reveal

Greg finally looks down at the rope tying him to Jesus on the raft.

Except — there is no rope.

Just proximity. Because the yoke was never a wooden tool. It was relationship.

“My way isn’t effortless. But it is unforced.”
“Let the river do the carrying.”

Jesus TV

“Easy, Like Watching TV — Jesus TV: ‘I will give you JTV.’”

It felt as though Jesus had stepped inside me, taken hold of my senses, and begun curating reality itself.

Using my own body as His instrument, He placed visions before my eyes — scenes, symbols, and stories — like an endless cosmic broadcast only I could see.

“Feed your head, Greg.”

The stream continued hour after hour — a divine binge-watch He called Jesus TV.
