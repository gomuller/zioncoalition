Begin With The Breath

Pause this movie and hold your breath as long as you can.

Now pause it again and run full speed five times around the outside of your home. Or, if you prefer, simply hold your breath again until it hurts.

Then stop. Catch your breath.

This small exercise is meant to remind you, in your body, how needy you are of breath — of The Breath — every moment. Especially under pressure. Especially when life becomes difficult to breathe through. Especially when obstacles literally and figuratively take your breath away.

What Is The Breath?

The Breath is the single most vital part of My Jesus-Greg WORLD.

Returning to The Breath — returning to Jesus, which is repentance — is the most basic foundation practice in the universe.

The Breath is plain and simple. And at the same time, it is mysterious, complex, and endlessly rich.

Seeking, finding, and returning to The Breath will be a lifetime adventure. It is a pursuit of mastery. It is the essence of the universal quest we are on.

Enter Jon Kabat-Zinn

So take a deep breath. Let us begin our deep dive into The Breath with Mr. Jon Kabat-Zinn — a fellow sojourner on earth, and a person who has spent much of his life observing and teaching the practice of returning to The Breath.

Jesus has had Jon present mindfulness in a secular, scientific framework, so people of any religion — or no religion — can practice it.

Jon’s little movie gives us key instructions that can change how we think about returning to The Breath. Not only the breath in our lungs, mouth, and nostrils, but also The Breath in its many other approximations — especially Jesus, the Breath of Life.

If you let it, this training can begin a process of decolonization and recolonization. It can change how you think about The Breath, the Spirit inside you, and eventually everything in your universe — your Jesus WORLD.

Two Things To Notice Before Watching

1. The Karate Kid Lesson

When Mr. Miyagi asked Daniel to paint the fence, wax the car, sand the deck, and catch the fly, were those instructions really about painting, waxing, sanding, and catching?

Yes. No. Actually, yes and no.

Returning to The Breath is like that. It is both obvious and hidden. Known and unknown. Bland and mysterious. Like breathing itself, it is an in-and-out, yes-and-no interaction.

Notice that Mr. Miyagi tells Daniel to “breathe in, breathe out” while doing the movements. Why does The Breath need to blend with the training?

2. Meditation As Love

In Jon’s training, listen for this sentence:

“For me, meditation is an act of love.”

To me, those are the most important words Jon says.

Returning to The Breath is not merely a technique. It is love. Jesus is love. And love is the deepest expression of The Breath.

This Breath-Love connection is a grand key to activating the advanced leapfrog technology of a Jesus WORLD. It is key to being released from the colonizing powers of Babylon. It is key to bringing more love, more Breath, and MORE Jesus into food, clothing, shelter, scenes, hobbies, relationships, jobs, and everything else in our worlds.
